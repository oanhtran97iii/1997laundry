Laundry in Bui Vien: Avoid Common Tourist Traps & Scams

October 24, 2026 5 min read By 1997 Expat Care Team

Staying in the Bui Vien backpacker area? Make sure you check these 3 simple rules to avoid common laundry scams and protect your clothes.

Bui Vien Walking Street in District 1, Ho Chi Minh City is famous for its night-life, street food, and budget hostels. However, as a high-traffic tourist zone, it is also home to common street scams, including rigged laundry practices. Here is how you can stay safe:

1. Rigged Scales & Weight Inflations

Some street-side laundry vendors use modified scales that show double your actual laundry weight. Always verify the weight yourself or choose a premium provider that sends you a photograph of the scale with your bag on it before starting. At 1997 Premium Laundry, we send transparent weight confirmation photos directly to your WhatsApp/Zalo.

2. Mixed Water and Unhygienic washing

To save costs on water, electricity, and detergents, cheap local stands often wash clothes from multiple customers together in one large load. This transfers odors, bacteria, and lint between clothes. Always ask for a guarantee of 100% separate machines for your order.

3. Hidden 'Express' Surcharges

Many stands list a cheap price (e.g. 20,000 VND/kg) on their sign boards, but double the price when you collect your clothes, claiming you needed an "express wash".
