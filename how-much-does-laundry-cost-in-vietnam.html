When traveling or living in Vietnam, laundry is one of those simple necessities that can either be extremely cheap or surprisingly expensive depending on where you go. In this guide, we break down the three primary pricing structures you will find in major cities like Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi.

1. Traditional Per-Kilogram Street Shops (30,000 VND - 40,000 VND/kg)

Local neighborhood laundry shops typically charge by weight. The standard rate is between 30,000 VND and 40,000 VND ($1.20 - $1.60 USD) per kilogram for wash, dry, and fold. Usually, there is a minimum order weight (usually 3.5kg) to ensure your clothes are washed in a dedicated machine rather than mixed with others.

2. Express Same-Day and 4-Hour Packages (180,000 VND - 220,000 VND flat)

For busy travelers or tourists with tight checkout schedules, same-day and 4-hour express packages are highly popular. Rather than charging by the kg, these are sold as flat-rate bundles (e.g., 180,000 VND for up to 5kg base Same-Day, or 220,000 VND for 4-Hour Express). This guarantees instant allocation of dedicated machines and priority checkout delivery.

3. In-House Hotel Laundry Services ($5.00 - $12.00 per piece)

Premium hotels charge per individual item. A single t-shirt can cost $6 USD to wash, and a pair of jeans can cost $10 USD. A single bag of laundry can easily run up to $120 - $150 USD! Using outside services like 1997 Premium Laundry offers up to 80% savings with convenient lobby reception pickup.
